Day 1

Show Up

The hardest part is starting. Not the workout, the decision to show up. You made it. That already puts you ahead of most people. Now let's move.

Mindset

Today is not about being perfect. It's about proving to yourself that you can. One session. One decision. That's it.

Workout: Full Body Circuit
Warm up 5 min: march in place, arm circles, hip rotations
3 rounds · 40 sec work · 20 sec rest · 90 sec between rounds
Cool down 5 min: child's pose, hip flexor stretch, deep breathing
1. Bodyweight Squats
Feet shoulder width, chest up, sit back into it
2. Push-Ups (or knee push-ups)
Full range, chest to floor, lock out at the top
3. Reverse Lunges (alternating)
Step back, back knee almost touches the floor
4. Dumbbell Rows (or resistance band)
Squeeze at the top, control on the way down
5. Glute Bridges
Drive through your heels, squeeze hard at the top
6. Plank Hold
Straight line from head to heels. Breathe.
Nutrition Focus: Protein First

Today's only rule: eat protein at every meal. Eggs, chicken, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, protein shake. Whatever works. Aim for 20 to 30 grams per meal.

Don't overthink food today. Prioritize protein and drink your water. That's it.

Find Your Protein Goal
grams of protein per day
Spread it out. Aim for 20 to 40g per meal rather than hitting it all at once.
Easy sources: chicken breast, eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, lean beef, protein shakes, tofu.
Consistency beats perfection. Getting close every day beats hitting it perfectly twice a week.
Day 2

Fuel Right

You showed up yesterday. Now we build. Today we talk about what you put in your body, because training hard and eating wrong will keep you stuck forever.

Mindset

Food is fuel, not the enemy. Stop punishing yourself for what you ate last week. Today is a fresh start. We're building habits, not chasing perfection.

Workout: Lower Body + Core Circuit
Warm up 5 min: leg swings, hip circles, bodyweight good mornings
3 rounds · 40 sec work · 20 sec rest · 90 sec between rounds
Cool down 5 min: seated hamstring stretch, pigeon pose, quad stretch
1. Sumo Squats
Wide stance, toes out. Feel it in your inner thighs.
2. Romanian Deadlifts
Hinge at the hips, slight knee bend. Feel the hamstrings stretch.
3. Step-Ups (chair or bench)
Drive through the heel of the elevated foot
4. Dead Bug
Lower back stays pressed to the floor the entire time
5. Side Lunges (alternating)
Sit into the working leg, chest up
6. Mountain Climbers
Control the pace. This is core work, not just cardio.
Nutrition Focus: Build a Real Plate
Half plate
Veggies or fruit
Quarter plate
Lean protein
Quarter plate
Complex carbs
Examples
Rice, oats, sweet potato

You don't need to count calories today. Just build the plate right. This one habit alone will change your body over time.

Day 3

Commit

Two days in. You're already ahead of the version of you that downloaded this and never started. Day 3 is about making a real decision. Are you doing this for real this time?

Mindset

Results don't come from 3 days. They come from what you decide to do after day 3. The people who transform are the ones who keep going when it stops feeling new. That's the work.

Workout: Full Body Strength Circuit
Warm up 5 min: jumping jacks, torso twists, inchworms
4 rounds · 35 sec work · 25 sec rest · 60 sec between rounds
You're a little stronger now. Push it.
Cool down 5 min: full body stretch, deep breathing
1. Goblet Squat
Hold dumbbell at chest. Elbows inside your knees at the bottom.
2. Push-Up to Shoulder Tap
After each rep, tap opposite shoulder. Don't let your hips rock.
3. Single Leg Glute Bridge (alternating)
One leg extended, drive through the grounded heel
4. Dumbbell Curl to Press
Curl up, press overhead. Control both directions.
5. Lateral Band Walks (or bodyweight side steps)
Stay low, stay wide. Feel the glutes the whole time.
6. Hollow Body Hold
Press lower back into the floor, arms overhead. Breathe.
Nutrition Focus: Hydration + Recovery

Today focus on water. Goal: half your bodyweight in ounces. 160 lbs = 80 oz of water. Add protein within 30 to 45 minutes after your workout to support recovery.

Most people are chronically dehydrated. Water alone will improve your energy, digestion, and performance more than any supplement.
